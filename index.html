PRIVACY POLICY · DRAFT

DataDost Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026 · Designed in alignment with India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023

⚠️ Beta notice: DataDost is currently in early beta testing. This policy describes our intended practices and design principles. Formal entity registration and full DPDP Act compliance mechanisms are in progress and will be completed before public launch.
Our Four Core Commitments
✓ We never store your original documents. Bank statements and PDFs are read and deleted immediately after analysis.
✓ We never sell your data to any third party — ever. No advertisers, no banks, no government without your explicit consent.
✓ We never share your financial information without your knowledge and consent.
✓ You can delete all your data instantly, anytime, with one click.
🤝 Sharing your data is entirely your choice
Uploading a document or asking DataDost a question is always voluntary, at your sole discretion, and reversible. By choosing to upload a document, you are giving DataDost explicit permission to read and parse that specific document for the sole purpose of answering your question. We do not access, request, or parse any data you have not actively chosen to share with us in that moment. You may stop sharing, delete what you've shared, or close your account at any time, with no obligation to continue.
1. What data we collect
We collect only what is necessary to provide the DataDost service: your name and email address (for your account), extracted financial insights from documents you upload (spending categories, totals, balances — never the raw document), and your questions and DataDost's responses within your session. We do not collect Aadhaar numbers, PAN numbers, bank account numbers, card numbers, or any government identification.
2. How we process your documents
When you upload a document, it is temporarily processed by our AI system (Anthropic Claude API) to extract financial insights. The raw document file is permanently deleted from our servers within seconds of processing. Only the extracted insights (e.g., "food delivery spend: ₹4,820", "closing balance: ₹3,820") are stored in your account — never the original PDF or image.
3. Where your data is stored
All user account data and financial insights are stored on servers located in India (Mumbai region, AWS ap-south-1) in compliance with the Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023. Your data does not leave Indian territory. All data is encrypted at rest using AES-256 encryption and in transit using TLS 1.3.
4. Your rights under DPDP Act 2023
You have the right to: access all data DataDost holds about you, correct any inaccurate data, delete all your data permanently, withdraw consent at any time, and nominate someone to exercise these rights on your behalf. To exercise any of these rights, email us at privacy@datadost.in or use the Delete Account option in your profile settings.
5. Third-party services
DataDost uses the following third-party services to operate: Anthropic (AI processing — your document content is transmitted to Anthropic's API for analysis, subject to Anthropic's privacy policy), Supabase (database and authentication — data stored in India region), Vercel (website hosting — static files only, no personal data), and Razorpay (payment processing — we never see your full card number). We do not use Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, or any advertising tracking tools.
6. Grievance Officer
As required under the DPDP Act 2023, our designated Grievance Officer can be contacted at: privacy@datadost.in. We will respond to all grievances within 30 days of receipt.
